# Merged Timeline: Real Longmont History vs. LoCo Pro Wrestling Canon

This timeline juxtaposes documented events in Longmont's history with the in-universe canon established on LoCo Pro Wrestling's "Longmont Lore" page.

- Entries marked **R** reflect historically documented events in Longmont's development.
- Entries marked **F** originate from LoCo Pro Wrestling's "Longmont Lore" page and are fictional within the real world but treated as truth inside the LoCo Pro universe.

| Jan 5, 1912 | R | Longmont's hydroelectric plant delivers its first power; the city declares itself the **"City of Lights"** and provides residents with free porch lights. |
| 1912 | R | Construction of a hydropower plant on the North St. Vrain provides reliable electric power. |

## Notes and Integration Guidance

1. **Documented vs. Canon:** Entries marked R reflect historically documented events in Longmont's development. Entries marked F originate from LoCo Pro Wrestling's "Longmont Lore" page and are fictional within the real world but treated as truth inside the LoCo Pro universe. Keeping these categories distinct prevents conflating history with performance narrative.

2. **Narrative Threads:**
   - The early planning and settlement of Longmont (1870–71) aligns with the fictional "Battle for Main Street," enabling storylines about land rights and class conflict.
   - The municipal power struggle (1911–12) provides real background for LoCo Pro's "City of Lights Championship" and the metaphor of community ownership.
   - Floods in 2013 and subsequent resilience efforts can inform modern "Water Is Power" arcs, while the canon's 2017 Opera House bout underscores wrestling as a language of resistance.

3. **Use of Citations:** Real events are cited from the City of Longmont history page and the city's power-utility history PDF. Fictional events are cited using screenshot identifiers from the LoCo Pro history page. These citations ensure transparency when blending history with mythology.
