# The Final Word: Nicky Hyde

---

*His voice is a low, theatrical crawl that builds into a jagged edge.*

*Nicky requests the presence of The Rising at ringside. Those that are able, abide.*

---

> "Tonight, The Rising's flame will be extinguished all because of one man.
>
> Look at this room, Zeak. Look at the gold leaf peeling off the ceiling and the red velvet soaked in a century of sweat. You tell these people, these good, desperate people, that you are their savior. You tell them that The Rising is the spark that will burn down the fortress we've built.
>
> But tell me, 'Hero'… did you ever tell them who built the fortress in the first place?"

*Hyde slowly unfurls the yellowed, heavy parchment of the Charter.*

> "Lineage is a funny thing. You can try to wash the blood off your hands, but you can't wash it out of the foundation. See this signature at the bottom? The one dated 1881? That's a Pillar's name. But that's not just any Pillar's name. That name belongs to your Great-Grandfather. He didn't build an Opera House for the arts, Zeak. He built a cage. He ensured that in this city, in this room, only those with the 'Proper Name' would ever hold the keys."

*Hyde begins to pace — a manic, rhythmic step.*

> "But the sins of the father are boring. Let's talk about the sins of the son.
>
> Eight years ago, when the Pillars and the Dickens Opera House drafted their 'Exclusive Booking Clause,' the one that turned this ring into a private playground for The Pillars, who was sitting at the mahogany table? Who held the silver pen? Who looked at the contracts that shackled Anuka, that silenced Erza, that turned this sport into a corporate ledger and said, 'YES'?"

*He stops, pointing the Charter directly at Zeak like a weapon.*

> "It wasn't JT Staten. It wasn't me. **It was YOU, Zeak!** You didn't find the 'City of Lights,' you turned them off! You weren't fighting the system… **YOU WERE A PILLAR!** You signed the rights away for a seat at the table, and now that you've been kicked off the guest list, you want to play the Revolutionary?"

*Hyde lets out a sharp, jagged laugh that echoes in the Opera House.*

> "The Rising isn't a movement, you fraud. It's a guilt trip! And tonight, in the house your family built and the system you signed into law… we aren't just going to beat you. We're going to show these people that their 'Golden Boy' is just a Pillar who lost his coat!
>
> Welcome home, Zeak. Let's see how you like the cage you built!"

---

*Zeak slides into the ring, picks up the microphone, and in shock just says:*

> "It's all true. Every word."

*A collective gasp hits the room. Zeak finally looks up, but his gaze stays on The Rising.*

> "I sat at that table. I held that silver pen. And I told myself that if I was the one signing the papers, I could make the chains a little lighter. I told myself I could be a 'good' Pillar. I thought I could change the room from the inside.
>
> But you can't fix a poison by drinking it.
>
> I wasn't running from what I did. I was terrified. I was terrified that if I told the truth, the only people who ever saw me as more than a name on a contract would look at me the same way again. I didn't want to be the architect of your misery. I wanted to be the man who finally turned the lights back on.
>
> I'm not a hero. I'm a man who helped build a prison, and I've spent every day since trying to find the keys. You want to talk about lineage? Fine. My family built the walls. I signed the deeds. But that means I know exactly where the cracks are.
>
> I'm not fighting for my name tonight. I'm fighting because I'm the only one here who knows exactly how much this system deserves to die.
>
> I know it's a lot to process, but we've got a job to do tonight. Who is with me?"

*The Rising hesitate.*

*From the crowd, a voice shouts:*

> **"I AM!"**

*Niles Blood stands up and slides into the ring from the crowd. He takes the mic from Zeak.*

> "Listen to me, Zeak. I'm gonna be real with you. Usually, when you start talking about 'hope' and 'The Rising,' I want to go out back and find a black coffee just to wash the taste of all that sap out of my mouth. You guys? You're corny. Honestly, this whole team-spirit, power-of-friendship thing you've got going on? It's a Saturday morning cartoon. It's a cereal box commercial. It makes my skin crawl."

*He looks over at the Pillars, his eyes narrowing into cold slits.*

> "But man… **do I hate those guys.**
>
> I hate the manipulation. I hate the lawyer-speak. I hate the 'Founding Charters.' I hate the way they breathe. And yeah, you made a mistake. You sat at their table and you ate their food. But you just stood here in front of this whole city and bled to make it right. You owned it.
>
> In a town full of Pillars who lie until their hearts stop, you actually stood up and said, 'I was the problem.' That's not a Saturday morning cartoon, Zeak. That's being a man."

*Niles steps shoulder-to-shoulder with Zeak, speaking to The Rising.*

> "We've all made choices we'd like to burn. It's what you do when the lights come up that defines who you are."

*Niles turns his attention back to Zeak.*

> "So, if you'll have a guy who thinks your catchphrases are trash but thinks your mission is worth a few broken knuckles… I'm with you.
>
> Let's go kick some Pillar ass."

---

*The Rising rush into the ring, ready to fight The Pillars and support Zeak no matter what.*

*Everyone makes their way to the back — except for Zeak and JT Staten.*
