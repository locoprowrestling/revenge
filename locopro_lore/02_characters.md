# LoCo Pro Wrestling Lore – Character Profiles

---

## 1. Zeak Gallent – "The Picture of Talent"

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Lead babyface of The Rising.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Presentation:
- High-level in-ring talent, charismatic, confident.
- Framed as the wrestler who can carry Longmont's scene into a better future.

[IN-UNIVERSE] History:
- Zeak has a shadowed past he's not proud of, but believes he has moved on from.
- His family's great-grandfather signed a document in 1881 that helped the original Pillars lock down the Opera House as a cage of power — a fact Zeak is aware of but has never disclosed publicly.
- Eight years before Vendetta, Zeak himself sat at the table and signed the **Exclusive Booking Clause** that turned the Dickens Opera House into a private playground for The Pillars — shackling Anuka, silencing Erza, and freezing out honest competition. He told himself he was a "good Pillar" who could change the system from within.
- He was wrong, and it cost him everything. He left The Pillars and joined The Rising, but never confessed the truth about his role.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Battle at the Opera House:
- Zeak defeats Franky Gonzales in Round 1 of the City of Lights Tournament.
- Zeak ultimately wins the City of Lights Tournament at Battle at the Opera House.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Commissioner Adam Starling presents Zeak with the long-hidden **City of Lights Championship**, formally naming him the **first LoCo Pro Wrestling City of Lights Champion**.
- Match 5: Zeak defeats Codah Alexander with no stipulations, setting the trap — forcing JT Staten vs. Zeak to open the City of Lights Elimination Gauntlet.
- **The Final Word:** Nicky Hyde publicly confronts Zeak with the Charter and the Exclusive Booking Clause, exposing his past in front of the Opera House crowd.
- Zeak confirms every word is true. In one of the most significant confessions in LoCo Pro history, he admits his role and declares he is fighting not for his name, but because he knows exactly how much this system deserves to die.
- Niles Blood, a stranger, becomes the first to stand with him.
- **Gauntlet:** Zeak opens the match against JT Staten. Survives a 3-on-1 situation. Eliminates Dean Mercer in a miracle spot. Eliminates the Pillar's random fan entrant, and is Eventually eliminated by Nicky Hyde.

## 2. Cody Devine – "Cloud King"

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Heel, public frontman of The Pillars.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Traits:
- Smug strategist and manipulator.
- Loves exploiting loopholes and fine print.
- Treats other people's relationships and careers as expendable tools.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Battle at the Opera House – Round 1:
- Booked to wrestle **Erza Menagerie Tinker**.
- Produces a signed contract allowing him to substitute any wrestler into his first-round match.
- Forces **Anuka Gutierrez** (Erza's boyfriend) to compete in his place, turning the match into an emotional weapon.
- Cody stays at ringside pulling the strings and takes credit for the advancement.

## 3. JT Staten

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Heel mastermind and real power behind The Pillars.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Traits:
- Treats wrestlers as assets in a portfolio.
- Prefers drinking at the bar and signing checks to actually getting in the ring.
- Uses money and contracts to make problems go away.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Battle at the Opera House – Round 1:
- Originally scheduled to face **Major Glory**.
- Produces his own substitution contract letting him send in a proxy.
- Sends **"All Business" Dean Mercer** to wrestle in his place while Staten heads to the bar.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Match 1: Claims his substitution clause is still in effect. Substitutes **Hussy Steele** in to face Anuka — only for ERZA to counter with the Ol' Switcharoo.
- Gauntlet: Opens the match against Zeak Gallent. Survives the entire gauntlet through corruption, leverage, and a corrupt referee (secured when Mercer won Match 2).
- Uses brass knuckles on Johnny Crash while the corrupt ref is "distracted."
- Benefits from **Carter Cash's** chair shot on Crash.
- Pins the unconscious Crash to win the City of Lights Elimination Gauntlet.
- **JT Staten wins Vendetta at the Opera House for The Pillars.**

## 4. "Pretty Boy" Franky Gonzales

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Heel, Pillars member.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Backstory:
- Second-generation talent.
- His father is a powerful Pillars-aligned figure, trapping Franky in a web of expectations and obligations.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Outside the ring:
- One half of **Pretty Great** alongside Michael Avalon.
- Together they are the **Primos Premier Pro Wrestling Tag Team Champions** and hold 2/3 of the **CSW/CSC Trios Championships**.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Battle at the Opera House – Tournament:
- Faces **Zeak Gallent** in Round 1, Pillars vs Rising showcase.
- Wrestles a strong match but loses to Zeak.
- Post-match, Zeak offers him a chance to leave The Pillars.
- Franky refuses, obviously conflicted but still Pillars-aligned.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Match 3: Faces his tag partner **Michael Avalon** in a contest where the stakes are JT Staten's Substitution Clause. Titles and partnership don't matter when the bell rings.
- Gauntlet: Enters at #10. Immediately punished and eliminated by Staten, Hyde, and Codah — thrown over the top rope as retribution for his earlier failure.

## 5. "The Stupendous One" Nicky Hyde

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Monster heel, Pillars-aligned.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Presentation:
- Masked, theatrical, vicious presence.
- Offense emphasizes cruelty and spectacle.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Battle at the Opera House – Tournament:
- Booked against **Johnny Crash** in Round 1.
- Ambushes Crash before the match, injuring him badly enough that he cannot compete.
- **Carter Cash** runs in and volunteers to replace Crash.
- Hyde defeats Carter decisively, cementing himself as a terrifying force and advancing in the tournament.
- Semi-final: Faces **Zeak Gallent**. Hunts shortcuts and rhythm breaks throughout; Zeak pushes for pace and a fair contest. Hyde's hubris backfires — he is counted out and **Zeak advances**. Hyde then attempts to assault the referee, only to be laid out by Zeak to close the first half of the show.
- City of Lights Battle Royale: Eliminates Johnny Crash and ERZA before being eliminated by Major Glory.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Match 4: Defeats **Carter Cash** in a Submissions Only rematch. Earns **The Final Word** — the right to address the Opera House crowd before the main event.
- **The Final Word promo:** Unfurls the yellowed Charter. Exposes Zeak's family history (great-grandfather built the cage in 1881) and Zeak's own signing of the Exclusive Booking Clause. Tries to destroy Zeak's moral authority before the gauntlet begins.
- Gauntlet: Enters at #5, begins a 3-on-1 assault on Zeak. Eliminates Zeak with a pinfall. Later eliminated by a double-team from Johnny Crash and Niles Blood.

## 6. Johnny Crash

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Babyface, allied with The Rising.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Traits:
- Punk-rock aesthetic and energy.
- A "human car wreck" who both dishes out and absorbs massive punishment.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Battle at the Opera House:
- Taken out by Nicky Hyde's pre-match assault before their scheduled Round 1 match.
- His temporary removal opens the door for Carter Cash to step in.
- Returns, injured, to fight side by side with The Rising in the City of Lights Battle Royale.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Match 2: Falls Count Anywhere Street Fight vs. Dean Mercer. Loses. The Pillars gain the right to choose the gauntlet's referee.
- Post-match: Codah Alexander and Hussy Steele rush the ring and put a beatdown on Crash. Saved by **Niles Blood**, who rushes in from the backstage, delivers a jarring kick to Codah, and helps Crash to his feet.
- Gauntlet: Enters at #11. Eliminates Codah Alexander. Teams with Niles Blood to eliminate Nicky Hyde. Is the last Rising member standing against JT Staten.
- **The Betrayal:** As Crash begins to stir after being dropped by Staten's brass knuckles, Carter Cash's music hits. Carter enters with a steel chair — seemingly the cavalry — then smashes Crash with it and walks away in silence.
- Pinned by JT Staten. The Rising loses Vendetta.

## 12. Adam Starling – "The Commissioner" / formerly "The Vigilante"

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Officially neutral Commissioner. Secretly a longtime anti-Pillars operative.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Public identity:
- Measured, disciplined, neutral authority figure.
- Speaks like a custodian of order — a man above factional warfare.
- Presents himself as here to preserve balance, enforce rules, and protect LoCo Pro Wrestling's integrity.
- That is the performance.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Hidden truth:
- Before he was Commissioner Adam Starling, he was **The Vigilante**.
- He stood inside the original 2017 LoCo Pro Wrestling conflict at the Dickens Opera House — the foundational war between The Rising and The Pillars of Mayhem.
- He watched **Cormac Battle** win the final against JT Staten.
- He watched the Pillars bury that result through paperwork rather than combat.
- On August 31, 2017, **Eustus Gallent VI** signed the **Dickens Opera House Wrestling Booking Exclusively Arrangement**, granting the Pillars exclusive rights to professional wrestling at the Opera House — then stopped booking wrestling there entirely, freezing Cormac's victory and making sure it could never grow into legacy.
- That theft became the central wound of Adam's life.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Origins:
- After 2017, Adam continued wrestling alongside **Thom Ingram** as the tag team **SpaceTime**.
- His bitterness over Cormac's buried victory slowly poisoned everything.
- He turned on Thom and destroyed SpaceTime's tag team championship dreams.
- A **Cult** swept up the vulnerable pair and drove them further apart. Thom was defeated and banished to the Netherrealm. To return, he shed his Cult identity and eventually left.
- Before Thom left, he left Adam one last voicemail: *"Call me if you need anything."*
- By 2020, Adam retired from active wrestling — not from peace, but from strategic surrender. He could no longer fight the Pillars inside the ring alone.
- In 2021, Adam understood the Pillars for what they were: not just villains, but a **system** — one that survives through leverage, legitimacy, memory control, exclusivity, and paperwork.
- In 2022, he came across the old 2017 Times-Call article again. He called Thom. **He bought LoCo Pro Wrestling from Thom Ingram.** That was the first contract Adam Starling ever used as a weapon.

[IN-UNIVERSE] As Commissioner:
- Engineered to speak with institutional legitimacy, shape rules and consequences, preserve outcomes in public record, and force corruption into the open.
- Presents as neutral. Neutrality is camouflage.
- His true mission: destroy the Pillars' ability to decide what matters.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Opens the show by formally validating The Rising's prior tournament win.
- Reveals the long-hidden **City of Lights Championship**, recovered from within the Opera House itself.
- Presents the title to **Zeak Gallent**, formally naming him the first LoCo Pro Wrestling City of Lights Champion.
- Grants The Rising the right to choose stipulations for every match on the undercard.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Endgame:
- After Carter Cash's betrayal and JT Staten's gauntlet win, Adam enters the ring. He drops the Commissioner posture and speaks directly to Carter — revealing himself as The Vigilante, warning Carter not to let his fire consume him the way Adam's consumed him.
- Carter listens. Takes a beat. **Superkicks Adam.** Walks out.
- Adam does not get absolution. The truth does not undo the damage.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Key relationships:
- **Zeak Gallent** — Adam's most important test case. He sees Zeak partly as Cormac Battle corrected — the rightful victor he is determined not to lose this time.
- **Carter Cash** — Adam sees in Carter his own anger. He believes Carter's fire, if not tempered, will burn through everything the way his own did.
- **JT Staten** — The living continuation of the 2017 wound. Adam's entire architecture is built, in part, to make a Pillar loss finally stick.
- **Thom Ingram** — Former partner, casualty of Adam's bitterness, and the man whose one voicemail opened the door back into the fight.
- **Cormac Battle** — The ghost at the center of Adam's life. Every time Adam tries to make a victory matter, he is, in part, trying to answer what was done to Cormac.

## 13. Hussy Steele

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Heel. JT Staten's #1 enforcer. Pillars-aligned.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Traits:
- Physical, ruthless, and loyal to Staten's authority.
- Not a manipulator — a weapon. The fist at the end of Staten's arm.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Match 1: Substituted in by JT Staten to replace Anuka Gutierrez — only for ERZA's Ol' Switcharoo counter to put Anuka back in the fight. Hussy faces Anuka to determine Anuka's freedom and eligibility for the main event.
- Gauntlet: Enters at #13. The ring splits — Hussy vs. Crash, Blood vs. Staten.
- **The Electricity:** Hussy and Niles Blood battle their respective opponents into opposite corners. They both stop simultaneously — spin around ready to strike each other — and lock eyes. The world disappears. The electricity between them is real and undeniable.
- Staten seizes the opening, dumps Blood over the top rope while Hussy is transfixed.
- Hussy stands stunned, staring over the ropes at the fallen Niles — and is immediately eliminated by a recovering Johnny Crash.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Unresolved thread:
- The moment between Hussy and Niles Blood in the gauntlet is the most charged unresolved beat of the night. Neither acts on it. It is left hanging.

## 16. Niles Blood

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Independent. Listed as "The Rebels." Functionally Rising-adjacent by night's end.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Traits:
- Blunt, anti-sentimental, and deeply principled beneath the cynicism.
- Despises manipulation, lawyer-speak, founding charters, and institutional corruption.
- Has zero patience for The Rising's team-spirit ethos — and yet cannot ignore the integrity behind it when forced to see it clearly.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta at the Opera House:
- Match 2 post-match: Rises from his seat in the crowd — a man in leather pants — and rushes the ring when Codah and Hussy attack Johnny Crash. Delivers a jarring kick to Codah Alexander and helps Crash to his feet. Returns to his seat in the crowd as if nothing happened.
- **The Final Word:** When Zeak publicly confesses his past and asks "Who is with me?" — the Rising hesitate. Niles Blood stands from the crowd, slides into the ring, takes the mic, and delivers a speech that cuts through sentimentality to reach something real. He hates The Rising's corny team-spirit brand. He thinks Zeak's catchphrases are trash. But Zeak stood up in front of the city and said "I was the problem" — and in a town full of Pillars who lie until their hearts stop, that is being a man. Niles stands shoulder-to-shoulder with Zeak.
- Gauntlet: Enters at #12. Teams with Johnny Crash to eliminate Nicky Hyde. Has the electricity moment with Hussy Steele. Eliminated by JT Staten while distracted by that moment.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Unresolved thread:
- The electricity between Niles and Hussy Steele in the gauntlet is real, mutual, and completely unaddressed. It is the most charged personal undercurrent left dangling after Vendetta.

## 19. Cormac Battle

[IN-UNIVERSE] Alignment: Historical babyface. The buried victor.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Role:
- Won the original LoCo Pro Wrestling final at the Dickens Opera House in 2017, defeating JT Staten.
- His victory was immediately buried. The Pillars used an exclusivity contract — signed by Eustus Gallent VI on August 31, 2017 — to lock professional wrestling out of the Opera House entirely, ensuring Cormac's win had nowhere to grow into legacy.
- Cormac Battle is the ghost at the center of Adam Starling's hidden mission. Every time Adam tries to make a victory matter, he is answering what was done to Cormac.
