## 2. What "Vendetta" Means In-Universe

[IN-UNIVERSE] *Battle at the Opera House* established the war. **Vendetta at the Opera House** is where that war turns personal.

[IN-UNIVERSE] The word **vendetta** signals a shift in tone:

- less about proving who belongs in the bracket
- more about settling scores left open by the City of Lights Tournament
- more about retaliation, humiliation, and payback inside sacred ground

[IN-UNIVERSE] The Dickens Opera House remains the symbolic heart of Longmont. Any faction that controls the narrative inside that room controls how the city remembers itself.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta is not presented as a reset. It is the direct aftermath of the first Opera House war, with damaged relationships, faction grudges, and power plays carrying forward.

## 3. Rematch Clause And The Rising Rules

[IN-UNIVERSE] Commissioner **Adam Starling** opens Vendetta by explicitly validating The Rising's prior victory at Battle at the Opera House.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Between *Battle at the Opera House* and *Vendetta at the Opera House*, Starling also discovers the **City of Lights Championship** hidden away inside the Opera House itself.

[IN-UNIVERSE] During the Vendetta intro, Starling reveals that championship to the crowd and presents it to **Zeak Gallent** for overcoming impossible odds in the City of Lights Tournament and becoming the **first LoCo Pro Wrestling City of Lights Champion**.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Starling's opening statement therefore establishes five crucial canon points:

- The Rising **did** win the City of Lights Tournament.
- The long-hidden **City of Lights Championship** has been recovered from the Opera House and formally brought back into circulation.
- **Zeak Gallent** is recognized as the **first LoCo Pro Wrestling City of Lights Champion**.
- The Pillars have invoked a **rematch clause**.
- Because of that clause, **The Rising** gets to choose the rules and stipulations for the night's matches.
- Every match on the card has direct consequences for the **final match of the night**, the **City of Lights Elimination Gauntlet**.

[IN-UNIVERSE] This means Vendetta is not just a revenge show. It is a legal and narrative counterattack: The Pillars forced a rematch, The Rising weaponized the terms, and Zeak enters the night carrying a title literally recovered from the building both factions claim to represent.

## 4. Status Of The War Entering Vendetta

### 4.1 The Rising

[IN-UNIVERSE] **The Rising** enter Vendetta with moral momentum.

- **Zeak Gallent** won the City of Lights Tournament at Battle at the Opera House.
- Between shows, Adam Starling discovered the hidden **City of Lights Championship** inside the Opera House and awarded it to Zeak at Vendetta.
- Zeak is therefore not just referred to as the **City of Lights Champion** in current site copy; he is formally established as the **first LoCo Pro Wrestling City of Lights Champion**.
- The Rising proved that The Pillars can be beaten in the open, in front of the city, under the Opera House lights.
- Adam Starling's opening declaration gives The Rising the authority to shape the stipulations for the night.

[IN-UNIVERSE] That momentum is not the same as total control. The Rising may have won the night's defining prize, but they have not removed The Pillars' backstage leverage, money, or institutional reach.

### 4.2 The Pillars

[IN-UNIVERSE] **The Pillars** enter Vendetta wounded, but still dangerous.

- **Cody Devine** reached the tournament final through manipulation, substitution abuse, and outside interference.
- **JT Staten** remains the faction's real money power, willing to weaponize paperwork, loopholes, and hired violence.
- **Dean Mercer** won the City of Lights Battle Royale, proving The Pillars still have access to brutal, professional force.
- **Nicky Hyde** left a trail of physical damage that was never fully answered inside the tournament alone.

[IN-UNIVERSE] The Pillars are the kind of faction that treat embarrassment as something to be avenged, not accepted. Vendetta therefore reads as their attempt to reclaim narrative control after Zeak's signature win.

## 6. Confirmed Final Match Framework

### 6.1 Final Match Identity

[IN-UNIVERSE] The final match of Vendetta is explicitly named:

- **Final - City of Lights Elimination Gauntlet**

[IN-UNIVERSE] Adam Starling frames it as:

- **The Rising vs Every Pillar here tonight**

### 6.2 Confirmed Gauntlet Rules

[IN-UNIVERSE] The reviewed final-match document confirms these rules:

- only **4 competitors** can be in the ring at a time
- the **first 2 competitors** start
- **2 minutes later** a 3rd person joins
- **2 minutes later** a 4th person joins
- once the 4th competitor joins, **new competitors enter only as eliminations occur**, regardless of time

## 7. Confirmed Carryover Threads From Battle At The Opera House

### 7.1 Zeak Gallent's victory changes the landscape

[IN-UNIVERSE] Zeak's tournament win did more than give The Rising a trophy moment. It made him the clearest living symbol of Longmont's future.

- He beat Franky Gonzales in Round One.
- He survived Nicky Hyde in the semi-finals.
- He overcame **Cody Devine** and **Dean Mercer** in the final.
- He was later presented with the recovered **City of Lights Championship** by Adam Starling.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Entering Vendetta, Zeak is not just another roster member. He is the benchmark The Pillars need to discredit or destroy.

### 7.2 Cody Devine still has unfinished business

[IN-UNIVERSE] Cody reached the finals, but his path was built on manipulation rather than dominance.

- He forced **Anuka Gutierrez** to replace him against **Erza Menagerie Tinker**.
- He benefited from interference and corruption against **Major Glory**.
- He failed to close the night as the face of victory for The Pillars.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta therefore leaves Cody with multiple open enemies and a damaged public claim to greatness.

### 7.3 JT Staten's system still exists

[IN-UNIVERSE] Staten never needed to pin anyone to shape the tournament.

- He used contracts to avoid direct risk.
- He sent Dean Mercer as a proxy.
- He remained a power broker rather than a front-line fighter.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta implies that Staten's influence is still active. Even after Zeak's win, the machine behind The Pillars has not been dismantled.

### 7.4 Dean Mercer remains a major threat

[IN-UNIVERSE] Dean Mercer lost in Round One to Major Glory, but then won the **City of Lights Battle Royale** and forced his way into the finals anyway.

[IN-UNIVERSE] That contradiction is important:

- Mercer can be beaten in a straight match
- Mercer can still bulldoze through chaos and seize opportunity

[IN-UNIVERSE] Entering Vendetta, Mercer stands as proof that The Pillars do not need honor if they can simply keep sending violence after the same people.

### 7.5 Nicky Hyde's damage was never fully settled

[IN-UNIVERSE] Hyde's tournament story was built on disruption and injury.

- He ambushed **Johnny Crash** before their scheduled match.
- He forced **Carter Cash** into an emergency replacement role.
- He left lasting physical and emotional damage in his wake before eventually falling to Zeak.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta naturally inherits that fallout. Hyde is the kind of threat that creates vendettas simply by surviving the first act.

### 7.6 Erza, Anuka, and Cody remain an unresolved wound

[IN-UNIVERSE] One of Battle at the Opera House's ugliest turns was Cody forcing Anuka into Erza's match.

- Erza was denied a fair fight.
- Anuka was coerced into betraying someone he loved.
- Cody turned intimacy into leverage and spectacle.

[IN-UNIVERSE] The current lore set does not document a clean resolution to that triangle. Entering Vendetta, it remains one of the most personal and emotionally volatile threads in the promotion.

### 7.7 Franky Gonzales is still conflicted

[IN-UNIVERSE] Franky lost clean to Zeak and was offered a path away from The Pillars.

[IN-UNIVERSE] He refused, but not from confidence. He refused from entanglement.

[IN-UNIVERSE] That makes Franky important going into Vendetta:

- he is still Pillars-aligned
- he has already been given a moral exit ramp
- he remains one of the most obvious fracture points inside the faction

## 8. Segment Anchors And Promo Context

### 8.1 Intro - Adam Starling

[IN-UNIVERSE] Adam Starling's intro is not just housekeeping. It is a declaration of legitimacy.

- He reveals the long-hidden **City of Lights Championship**.
- He presents that championship to **Zeak Gallent**.
- He reaffirms the City of Lights Tournament result.
- He treats the rematch clause as binding.
- He gives The Rising the power to choose stipulations.
- He declares that every match will shape the **City of Lights Elimination Gauntlet**.

[IN-UNIVERSE] This frames Vendetta as a structured act of retaliation under official sanction rather than pure chaos. Starling is not merely announcing matches; he is restoring a hidden symbol of the city and placing it on Zeak's shoulder before the war continues.

### 8.2 The Final Word - Nicky Hyde

[IN-UNIVERSE] The "Final Word" segment gives Nicky Hyde a major ideological promo before the gauntlet.

[IN-UNIVERSE] In the reviewed page, Hyde:

- requests the presence of **The Rising** at ringside
- claims The Rising's flame will be extinguished because of "one man"
- unfurls a yellowed **Charter**
- argues that the Pillars' claim to the Opera House and its keys is older than the modern faction war
- specifically ties that bloodline claim to **Zeak Gallent's great-grandfather**
- shifts from the "sins of the father" to the "sins of the son"
- invokes the **Exclusive Booking Clause** from eight years earlier
- links that clause to the same contract culture that shackled **Anuka** and silenced **Erza**

[IN-UNIVERSE] Even without the later pages of the segment, the canon function is clear: Hyde is trying to poison Zeak's symbolic purity by arguing that Zeak's family line is entangled with the very system The Rising claims to oppose.

## 9. What Vendetta Is Confirmed To Be About

[IN-UNIVERSE] Based on the public story copy plus the reviewed event documents, Vendetta is confirmed to center on the following stakes:

- **The Rising vs The Pillars** is still the central war.
- Longmont's identity is still framed as the real prize.
- The recovered **City of Lights Championship** turns Zeak's prior tournament win into a formal civic title.
- The Pillars invoked a legal rematch structure, but The Rising seized control of the stipulations.
- Every undercard match carries a direct consequence into the final.
- The final is not a standard singles bout but a faction-loaded **City of Lights Elimination Gauntlet**.
- The aftershocks of the City of Lights Tournament are still active and unresolved.

[IN-UNIVERSE] Vendetta is therefore best understood as:

- the first real revenge chapter after Zeak's breakthrough win
- the night when tournament fallout turns into grudge violence
- a test of whether The Rising can convert one heroic victory into lasting power
- a test of whether The Pillars can use wealth, contracts, and brutality to drag the city backward again
- a night where old contracts, bloodline claims, and Opera House legitimacy are all dragged back into the light
